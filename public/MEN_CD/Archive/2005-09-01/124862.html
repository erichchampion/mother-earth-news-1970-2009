Tractor Safety Is No Accident

September/October 2005

Tractors and riding mowers make life easier, more enjoyable andproductive, but they also make it more dangerous. One person diesin a tractor accident nearly every day in the United States. Tomake country life safer, follow these tips for tractor use:


  2. Know the location and properuse of all controls.

  4. Before starting a tractor, inspect for leaks,flat tires and other hazards.

  6. Never mow near bystanders.

  8. Before dismounting, switch off the engine andwait for the tractor and PTO-powered implements to come to acomplete stop.

  10. Never carry riders, especially children.

  12. Avoid bystanders, holes, depressions, bumps,stumps, steep slopes and other hidden hazards.

  14. Stay as far away from a ditch as the ditch isdeep.

  16. When turning on a slope, turn downhill. Keepside-mounted equipment on the uphill side.

  18. Slow down when turning, crossing slopes ordriving on slick surfaces.

  20. Keep the front-end loader low to the ground.

  22. On the highway, lock brake pedals together. Useheadlights and a slow-moving vehicle emblem.

  24. Wear hearing protection and seat belts, andremove the ignition key when you're finished.

  26. Use a Roll-over Protective Structure (ROPS) ifpossible.
