Mom's 1984 "Wishbook" Guide To Herb Suppliers

January/February 1984

We've gotten you well started on your 1984 garden, we hope, with our list of general seed companies on page 61 . . . but what's a garden-or life, for that matter-without herbs? And although we admit we're prejudiced on the subject, we do urge you to explore the wonderful world of herbs and spices if you haven't already done so. To help get you going, we've compiled a list of 24 companies that specialize in this field. Some deal primarily in plants and seeds while others sell dried herbs, books, and herbal products (culinary blends, essential oils, medicinal preparations, personal care products, potpourris, and so on). Along with each company's main offerings, the cost-and occasionally, the special nature of its catalog has been indicated.

Please note that some of these companies carry other items, such as ornamental and culinary plants and seeds, and books and tools related to general gardening, as well as herbs. Furthermore, many of the major seed companies (such as Parks and Burpee) also offer herbs.

And now, happy shopping . . . and growing!
