Metal Building Warning

April/May 2002

COUNTRY LORE

Please inform your readers who are thinking about buying a do-it-yourself metal building to ask for the construction manual before they buy.

Some manufacturers are misleading in their ads. By studying the manual first, one can find out if the manufacturer's idea of do-it-yourself is the same as yours. This will help prevent unexpected headaches and expenses, such as having to hire extra people to help put the building together. Also, potential buyers can check out the metal building's foundation requirements. They can be more complicated and expensive than you think. Take this advice from someone who has learned the hard way.


CURTIS JOHNSON
 Bucyrus, Ohio
